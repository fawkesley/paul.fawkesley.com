Why Globaleaks?

I'm passionate about human rights and internet freedom, and how we can use technology to organise and to hold power to account. I'm a volunteer at the Internet Freedom Festival and I've done some public speaking and technical writing in this area.

I've stuck with free software and decentralised tools like GnuPG for almost two decades - despite their shortcomings - because it's essential that individuals have tools that aren't controlled by companies and governments.

My voluntary work helping PGP users has connected me to lots of interesting journalists, lawyers and other activists and it's wonderful to support these people in their important work.

Working on the Globaleaks whistleblowing software would allow me to do what I do best, in the exact area that I want to do it.

Relevant experience

Expirybot (tools for PGP users), side project

2017 - present

Expirybot is an open source service to makes life easier for PGP users. Currently it reminds you when your key is about to expire, and it monitors the keyservers for potential impersonation.

As I'm limited on time, I develop Expirybot by continually asking for feedback, listening, then building the next improvement. I love spending time really understanding problems and people's experience, then testing a minimal solution. If it works, I develop it further.

Footnote: Engineer / Developer / Product Person

I'm an Engineer at heart - I'm happiest with my head in a terminal, creating code that's readable, tested and standards compliant. I'm happy with Linux, deployment, CI and running production services.

I also love deeply understanding my users and using that to inform the product. I love testing things with people and seeing where my assumptions are wrong, then refining the assumptions.

I find this combination of developer and product manager can be powerful, allowing me to build and iterate things that are genuinely useful to people.
